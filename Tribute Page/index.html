    Jobs
    I find it incredibly ironic that the day I learned how to use online library databases in my University from my Apple computer, and I took a study break to watch TV, that I learned of Steve's passing. I owe everything to this man, because without my personal computer, library research would be all but impossible. I remember saving all of my Christmas money and work money to buy my first iPod at age 16. It was the first generation video iPod. On long car trips, walks, a trip to Europe, I took it everywhere, and still take my nano to class and work. When I went to college, I chose a Mac because of its easy usage and great customer support. Four years later, I am still using this Mac, and tonight from the University of Minnesota I am writing and researching my college honors thesis. I don't have an iPhone, iPad, or many of the gadgets others use at this point in time; I'm quite old fashioned and prefer to read material books, not much for touch screens, etc. But it wasn't until today how I learned just how revolutionary the digital world can be, and I will always remember that Steve innovated my favorite calming device and researching tool. Even if I wasn't the nerdiest, tech-savvy girl out there, Steve had something to offer. Thanks so much for making my college life easier, less-stressful, and for opening a larger perspective of the world.

    Janelle
